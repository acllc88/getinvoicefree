What Makes a Good Invoice Template?

The best invoice templates share a few key features regardless of format:

  • Clear header — your business name and logo prominent at the top
  • Unique invoice number — for both your records and your client's accounts system
  • Itemised line items — each service or product on its own line with description, quantity, and rate
  • Automatic totals — subtotal, tax (if applicable), discount, and total due
  • Due date — not just issue date. Invoices without due dates get paid last.
  • Payment method — your bank details, PayPal, or Stripe link in the Notes field
  • Professional PDF output — never send editable Word or spreadsheet files to clients

How do I create an invoice in Word?

Open Microsoft Word, go to File → New, and search "invoice" in the template gallery. Word has several free invoice templates built in. Fill in your details, but remember you'll need to manually calculate all totals. Our online generator does this automatically and produces a better-looking PDF.

How do I create an invoice in Excel?

Excel has free invoice templates under File → New → search "invoice". Excel templates are better than Word because they can calculate totals automatically. However, they're harder to format as clean PDFs and don't work well on mobile. Our online generator handles both of those problems.

Are there free invoice templates for Google Docs?

Yes. Google Docs has free invoice templates in the template gallery. The main limitation is that they don't auto-calculate totals (you need Google Sheets for that) and they require a Google account. Our online generator works without any account.

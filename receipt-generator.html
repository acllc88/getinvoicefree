What Is a Receipt?

A receipt is a document confirming that payment has been received. Unlike an invoice — which requests payment — a receipt is issued after the money has been paid. It gives both the buyer and seller a permanent record of the transaction.

Receipts are essential for deposits, cash transactions, advance payments, and any situation where you need written proof that money changed hands.

Receipt vs Invoice vs Estimate — The Difference

  • Invoice — Sent before or after work to request payment. Says "you owe me X."
  • Receipt — Sent after payment is received to confirm it. Says "you paid X, here's your proof."
  • Estimate — Sent before work begins to outline expected costs. Not a payment request.

When Should You Issue a Receipt?

  • After receiving a deposit or advance payment
  • After any cash transaction
  • When a client requests written proof of payment
  • After the final invoice balance is paid in full
  • For retail or product sales
  • For event bookings and reservations

What to Include on a Receipt

  • Your business name, email, and phone number
  • Customer name
  • Unique receipt number (for your records)
  • Date payment was received
  • Payment method (cash, bank transfer, PayPal, etc.)
  • Itemised list of what was paid for
  • Total amount received (including any tax)
  • "Payment received" confirmation

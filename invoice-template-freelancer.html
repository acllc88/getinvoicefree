Tips for Freelancers to Get Paid Faster

💡

Always include a due date

Net 15 or Net 30 is standard. Invoices without due dates get paid last.

💡

Itemize your work clearly

Break down each deliverable separately. "Website design" is vague — "Homepage design, 2 revisions" is professional.

💡

Include your payment details

Bank transfer, PayPal, or Wise. Put it in the Notes field so clients know exactly how to pay you.

💡

Send as PDF

Never send Word or editable files. PDF looks professional and can't be accidentally changed.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to register a business to send a freelance invoice?

No. As a sole trader or independent contractor you can invoice under your own name. Just include your full name and address where the business details go.

What should I charge for late payment?

A common clause is 1.5% monthly interest on invoices unpaid after the due date. Add this to your Notes / Payment Terms field.

How do I invoice internationally as a freelancer?

Use our currency selector to switch between USD, EUR, GBP, and more. For international clients, specify the currency clearly and use Wise or PayPal for cross-border payments.
